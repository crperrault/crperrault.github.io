2091 [1804, Shropshire, Worcestershire, Hertfordshire]
At Coalbrook-dale, aged 60, Mr Mark Gilpin, one of the people called QUakers, and many years principal clerk to the Coalbrook-dale Company. Of the multitude which this miscellany records as having “gone down to the grave”, none perhaps possessed a superior claim to the esteem and regret of surviving acquaintance than this amiable character. His affability and meekness, his integrity and circumspection in all the walks of life, and above all his unaffected piety, were models to all around him; indeed by the persuasive influence of example, he may be said to have been as “preacher of righteousness,” and that not the least eloquent. As he lived, so he died, in peace and calmness, embalmed with the tears of his numerous offspring, by whom he was justly venerated, for he early taught their feet to tread the path of virtue and peace.

Email from Joanne Smith, curator of Ironbridge Gorge Museum Collection, 6/23/14

Hello Ray

Our former Senior Curator has been doing a bit of searching and has come up with the following information for you:

There is a letter from Abiah Darby, widow of Abraham II, written on 25th May 1773 describing the Great Slip that had occurred the day before (a major landslip about a mile from the Gorge). She asked Mark to write it for her, so clearly he was a trusted family friend. He describes her as 'Mistress'. Archive reference IGMT.1986.11813.

By 1778 Mark we know that he represented Thomas Addenbrooke, Secretary to the Iron Bridge Trustees at their meeting of 22nd January 1778 (IGMT.1999.1103, a photocopy of the original Trustees' Minute Book for 1775-99 which is in the Shropshire Records Office). Addenbrooke advised the Darbys on business matters. The same month Mark wrote to the Duke of Devonshire on behalf of the Dale Company about their order for a pumping engine (IGMT. E2011.587, a photocopy of the correspondence from the Boulton & Watt collection in Birmingham Reference Library). At this date he is clerk to the Company, but we don't know if he was yet the principal clerk as he was at the time of his death.

When Samuel Darby died in September 1796 he left Mark Â£100 in his will dated 14 June 1794.

Mark's death was recorded in the 'Monthly Magazine' for December 1804, No 5, Volume 18, page 459. The entry says "At Coalbrook-dale, aged 60, Mr Mark Gilpin, one of the people called Quakers, and many years principal clerk to the Coalbrookdale Company." However, the Coalbrookdale Quaker Burial Ground records show the date of burial as '12/3mo/1805'. The delay is hard to explain, unless the month and day have been transposed.

His son John died in 1805, but his other son Mark Junior, predeceased him, being buried in the Coalbrookdale Quaker Burial Ground on 2nd October 1799.

There is also a family tree of the Thomas family which branched out into the Gilpins, Rose and Crannage families. It is a Labouchere collection item of c 1992, LAB.11941.10 with annotations by William Gregory Norris.

We don't have a great deal of information but we can see if we are able to produce digital copies of some of the items for you.

I hope this helps.

Best wishes
