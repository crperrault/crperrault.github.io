A Note on the Stuart Family

Raymond Perrault

July 20, 2025

This note is intended to situate the Stuart family to descendants of Joseph Beaubien.

Joseph Beaubien (1865-1949), my great-grandfather, was the son of Louis Beaubien (1837-1915) and Suzanne Lauretta Stuart (1844-1936). She was known as mamère, and for many years lived in a house in Outremont attached to that of Joseph and his wife, Joséphine Larue.

Lauretta Stuart was the daughter of Sir Andrew Stuart (1812-1891) and Elmire Aubert de Gaspé (1817-1899). Elmire was the daughter of the seigneur and author Philippe Aubert de Gaspé.

Sir Andrew Stuart was a lawyer who became chief justice of the Superior Court of Quebec and interim administrator of the province during the illness of the Lt. Gov. JL Masson.

Sir Andrew was the son of Hon. Andrew Stuart (1740-1811) and Marguerite Dumoulin (1781-1838).

Hon. Andrew Stuart, son of Rev. John Stuart (1740-1811) and Jane Okill,  was also a lawyer, member of the Quebec Assembly and solicitor-general of Quebec.

Hon. Andrew’s brother, Sir James Stuart (1780-1853), also a lawyer, was solicitor general, attorney general and chief justice of Quebec.

Rev. John Stuart was born in Pennsylvania of Scots-Irish immigrants, Andrew Stuart (1698-1774) and Margaret Dinwiddie. He was a missionary in upstate New York before settling in Kingston.

The origins of Andrew Stuart are not known.
